Interpretations of Probability

Classical

The classical interpretation of probability deals with events that are equally likely. We count the number of possible outcomes, which we will call n, and then say that the likelihood of event i is 1/n.

Classical theory

Frequency

The basic idea here is that the probability of some event e is equilavent to the number of times we would expect to see e if we repeatedly ran some process that randomly might produce e.
